Topological Superconductors in One or Two Dimensions

Introducing the Bogoliubov-de-Gennes(BdG) Formalism for s-Wave Superconductors

We begin by introducing the mean-field formulation of the quasiparticle physics for a conventional s-wave Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) superconductor. We start with a simple metal with spin-degeneracy given by the single-particle Hamiltonian \begin{equation} H = \left( \frac{p^2}{2m}-\mu \right)I_{2\times 2} \end{equation} where `\mu` is the chemical potential defining the fermi surface

For the many-body system, the second-quatized Hamiltonian is \begin{equation} H = \sum_{\mathbf{p},\sigma} c^{\dagger}_{\mathbf{p},\sigma}\left(\frac{p^2}{2m}-\mu\right)c_{\mathbf{p},\sigma}\equiv \sum_{\mathbf{p},\sigma} c^{\dagger}_{\mathbf{p},\sigma}\epsilon(p)c_{\mathbf{p},\sigma} \end{equation} where \begin{equation} c^\dagger_{\mathbf{p},\sigma} \end{equation} creates a quasiparticle with momentum `\mathbf{p}` and spin `\sigma`

The many-body ground state of this Hamiltonian is \begin{equation} |\Omega\rangle = \prod_{\mathbf{p}:\epsilon(p)< 0}\prod_\sigma c^\dagger_{\mathbf{p},\sigma}|0\rangle \end{equation} `|0\rangle` is defined by \begin{equation} c_{\mathbf{p},\sigma}|0\rangle = 0 \end{equation} for all `\mathbf{p},\sigma`
